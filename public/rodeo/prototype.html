The Big Idea

The Soft Yes™ — meeting without the hard ask

Every dating app ends at the same cliff: two strangers must agree to meet, alone, on the strength of a chat window. That ask is heavy — especially for women. rodeo replaces the hard ask with a soft one. Local events flow right into the deck. Tapping “I think I'll go” isn't a date, isn't a like, isn't a commitment — it's interest in a place, not a person. But quietly, it puts compatible people at the same rodeo, the same honky-tonk, the same chili cook-off — in public, with friends, with exits.

🪢

How the loop closes

When Guy One taps “I think I'll go” on Friday's rodeo, that signal travels to the feeds of people who've already shown interest in him. They see the same event card — but theirs reads “Some folks you might take a shine to are going. You going?” One more tap and two people have a reason to be in the same arena, no like sent, no rejection risked, no pressure to pair off. If they spot each other there, great. If not, nothing was lost — they still had a good Friday night. The worst case of a Soft Yes is a fun evening out. That's a first for dating apps.

🔥Roadmap

The Campfire

Everyone who soft-yes'd an event joins a group chat that opens 24h before and closes 24h after. Group banter is lower stakes than DMs — and gives people a reason to say "I'm by the funnel cake stand."

📍Roadmap

Check-in Sparks

Check in at the venue and rodeo quietly tells you when someone compatible checks in too — with a one-tap "tip your hat" 🤠 wave that expires when the event ends. Spontaneity, fenced in by time and place.

👯Roadmap

Ride with a Posse

Soft-yes an event with friends. Groups meeting groups in public is the oldest, safest social pattern there is — we just put rails under it.

💃Roadmap

Save a Dance

At live-music events, a digital dance card: offer one two-step, no chat required. A dance is three minutes long and ends on its own — the perfect-size first meeting.

🐎Roadmap

The Two-Step Intro

When two people soft-yes the same event twice in a season, rodeo offers each a private, pre-written wave. Mutual context first, introduction second — the reverse of every other app.

Business Model

Free to ride. Paid by the trail.

No subscription, no swipe limits, no pay-to-be-seen. rodeo monetizes the edges of the experience — the places money doesn't get between two people.

REVENUE 01

🎪 Promoted Events

Venues, rodeos, and festivals pay to ride in the deck alongside the organic local calendar — clearly flagged "Sponsored." It's advertising users thank us for, because every event makes the product work better.

REVENUE 02

🔗 Outbound Interstitials

When a user taps out to a profile's Instagram or other link, the trip out passes one sponsored card. Brands reach a precisely western audience; users lose two seconds, not two dollars.

REVENUE 03

🎥 Rodeo Pro Media

Nine photos free, forever. Power users pay once for more: 10+ photos and video intros. Vanity upgrades — never visibility, never reach, never messages.

🤝

The promise: the app never charges you to meet someone.

That promise is the marketing. In a category infamous for paywalled affection, "free to love, supported by the town square" is a story local press, venues, and users all want to tell.
